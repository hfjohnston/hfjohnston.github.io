Education & Professional Experience

University of Dundee (2015-2020)

MSci Physics (2:1)

I completed my integrated Master's degree in Physics at the University of Dundee in 2020. My thesis, titled "Modelling Stellar Spots on Young, Accreting Stars," was supervised by Dr Aurora Sicilia-Aguilar. This research involved constructing star-spot models from synthetic photometric data and convolving them with astronomical observations to establish spot geometry and accretion structure in young, active stars.

University of Leeds (2020-2024)

PhD Candidature in Astrophysics

I completed my PhD at the University of Leeds under the supervision of Dr Olja Panić. My thesis, "Exploring Planet Formation around Host Stars of Different Masses," focused on pebble-driven core accretion planet formation modelling. My research investigated the trends and optimal conditions required for giant planet formation around intermediate-mass stars, utilizing Monte-Carlo sampling techniques and combining formation models with chemical models to investigate compositions of giant planets in view of the upcoming ARIEL space mission.

University of Exeter (2024-Present)

Research Fellow in Astrophysics

Since October 2024, I have been working as a Research Fellow at the University of Exeter, funded by the Royal Society, in the research group of Dr Sebastián Marino. My current project focuses on debris disc formation, specifically investigating large and wide discs formed via external photoevaporation through dust and gas evolution modelling using DustPy.

Research Highlights

Throughout my career, I have developed a comprehensive understanding of:

  • Giant Planet Formation: Investigating how giant planets form and migrate within protoplanetary discs around stars of different masses
  • Disc Dynamics: Modelling the evolution of planet-forming discs and their response to planetary perturbations and stellar radiation
  • Stellar Influence: Understanding how host star properties (mass, luminosity, evolution) affect disc structure and planetary formation efficiency
  • Observational Constraints: Connecting theoretical models to observations from current and future missions (ARIEL, JWST)
