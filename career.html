Education & Professional Experience

University of Dundee (2016-2020)

I grew up in Dundee, Scotland, where I went to school and later completed my MSci in Physics at the University of Dundee in 2020. My master thesis, supervised by Dr Aurora Sicilia Aguilar, focused on modelling spots in young, accreting stars. I used photometric models to determine whether stars had cool/hot spots.

University of Leeds (2020-2024)

I then moved to Leeds, UK, in 2020 to start my PhD at the University of Leeds under the supervision of Dr Olja Panic. My thesis consisted of modelling giant planet formation around intermediate-mass stars within protoplanetary discs. Through this work, I developed expertise in numerical modelling of planet-disc interactions and explored how planetary systems form in diverse stellar environments. This period was instrumental in shaping my research focus on the connection between planet-forming environments and the resulting planetary architectures.

University of Exeter (2024-Present)

In 2024, I moved to Exeter, UK, to work as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Exeter, in Dr Seba Mairno's research group. I am currently working on the dispersal of protoplanetary discs and the transition to debris disc systems. My current research continues to explore how planetary systems influence and are influenced by their surrounding disc environments.

Research Highlights

Throughout my career, I have developed a comprehensive understanding of:

  • Giant Planet Formation: Investigating how giant planets form and migrate within protoplanetary discs
  • Disc Dynamics: Modelling the evolution of planet-forming discs and their response to planetary perturbations
  • Stellar Influence: Understanding how host star properties affect disc structure and planetary formation efficiency
