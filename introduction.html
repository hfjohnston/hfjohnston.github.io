About me and my research

Hi! Welcome

I am a Scottish astrophysicist investigating the link between planet-forming environments and their resulting planetary systems. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Exeter in the United Kingdom.

Research Focus

My research centers on understanding how planets form in protoplanetary discs and how these planetary systems evolve over time. I'm particularly interested in:

  • Giant planet formation and migration
  • The evolution of planet-forming discs
  • The influence of host stars on planetary systems
  • Disc photoevaporation and dispersal

Passion for Science

My passion lies in understanding the complex processes that shape planetary systems like our own. By studying the formation of planets around different types of stars, we can better understand the origins of our Solar System and the potential for planetary systems throughout the galaxy.
